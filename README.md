# Design, Fabrication, and Flight Testing of a Fixed-Wing UAV

## Project Overview
.

The aircraft was not limited to a CAD study. The team completed the full engineering cycle:

- mission and requirement analysis;
- conceptual, preliminary, and detailed aircraft design;
- aerodynamic and stability analysis;
- CAD development and engineering drawings;
- material selection and manufacturing planning;
- fabrication and assembly;
- subsystem testing;
- flight testing and mission preparation.

The final aircraft was a lightweight, electrically powered fixed-wing UAV designed for aerobatic flight, internal payload carriage, and controlled payload release.

## Final Aircraft Configuration

The selected configuration used:

- a high-wing monoplane layout;
- a fuselage-mounted tractor propeller;
- a conventional horizontal and vertical tail;
- a tapered wing;
- electric propulsion;
- integrated landing gear;
- a servo-operated payload-release mechanism.

The configuration was selected through a weighted comparison of competing aircraft layouts, propulsion arrangements, wing positions, wing geometries, tail layouts, and landing-gear options.

## Mission Requirements

The aircraft was developed for three competition missions:

### Mission 1 — Aerobatic Flight Without Payload

The UAV was required to take off within the specified runway length and complete circuit patterns, clockwise and counter-clockwise aileron rolls, and an inside loop.

### Mission 2 — Flight With Internal Payload

The aircraft carried a secured 200 g internal payload while completing circuit patterns and an inverted fly-pass.

### Mission 3 — Payload-Drop Mission

The UAV carried separate payloads and released them individually over a target area. The release system used two servos and supported controlled payload deployment.

## Engineering Design Process

### Conceptual Design

The team compared several configurations, including monoplane and biplane layouts, different propulsion arrangements, high-, mid-, and low-wing positions, multiple tail configurations, and alternative landing-gear layouts.

### Preliminary Design

The preliminary design stage included:

- propulsion-system selection;
- wing loading and geometry sizing;
- fuselage sizing;
- horizontal- and vertical-tail sizing;
- control-surface sizing;
- payload integration;
- weight and performance estimation.

### Detailed Design

The detailed stage defined the external geometry, internal structure, component layout, avionics, propulsion system, control surfaces, and payload-release mechanism.

## Aerodynamic and Stability Analysis

The aircraft was evaluated through:

- airfoil comparison and selection;
- wing and tail CFD analysis;
- pressure and velocity-contour assessment;
- lift and drag evaluation;
- level-flight performance calculations;
- thrust and power requirements;
- climb, endurance, range, and glide analysis;
- DATCOM-based stability-derivative estimation;
- longitudinal, lateral, and directional stability assessment.

The analyses were used to refine the geometry before fabrication and to check that the aircraft could meet the competition missions.

## Structural Design and Materials

The airframe combined lightweight materials with local reinforcement:

- **Depron:** wing skin and lightweight aerodynamic surfaces;
- **Thermocol:** fuselage shell and selected external surfaces;
- **Balsa wood:** spars, longerons, bulkheads, and local reinforcement;
- **Carbon-fiber rod:** structural stiffening and resistance to bending;
- **Epoxy and CA adhesive:** assembly and joint reinforcement;
- **Heat-shrink covering and surface finishing:** aerodynamic smoothing and protection.

This material combination produced a lightweight semi-monocoque structure suitable for fabrication with manual tools.

## Manufacturing and Fabrication

The aircraft was physically manufactured using:

- hand cutting and trimming;
- hot-wire cutting of thermocol;
- sanding and surface shaping;
- spar and bulkhead assembly;
- adhesive bonding;
- carbon-fiber reinforcement;
- control-surface installation;
- avionics and propulsion integration;
- final alignment, smoothing, painting, and inspection.

The design report includes photographs of the wing, fuselage, empennage, and final aircraft during fabrication.

## Payload-Release Mechanism

The payload system was designed to release up to three payloads using two servos. The mechanism included:

- individual payload retention;
- manual or controller-triggered release;
- controlled release timing;
- locking provisions to reduce accidental deployment;
- pre-flight inspection and functional checks.

## Electronics and Control System

The aircraft integrated:

- RC transmitter and receiver;
- metal-gear servos;
- brushless electric motor;
- electronic speed controller;
- LiPo battery;
- SpeedyBee F405 Wing flight controller;
- ArduPilot-based flight-control functions;
- onboard sensing for attitude, heading, position, and airspeed.

## Testing

The testing program covered:

- propulsion and thrust testing;
- servo and control-surface response;
- transmitter and receiver communication;
- fail-safe operation;
- battery voltage and endurance;
- wing load testing;
- motor-mount vibration testing;
- structural inspection;
- centre-of-gravity checks;
- pre-flight checks;
- takeoff, landing, maneuverability, payload carriage, and payload-drop trials.
